About


I am a Masters student in CS at the University of Chicago. In addition to class, I work on implementing new statistical methods in Python at the Center for Spatial Data Science, and migrating a new high performance computing cluster at the Center for Research Informatics. Last summer, I worked at the Computation Institute to improve a command line caching tool used by physicists. I expect to graduate in March of 2018, with experience and coursework in software engineering, iOS development, and machine learning.

Prior to graduate school, I was a data analyst in Nielsen's Marketing Analytics group, where I gained experience in predictive modeling, Bayesian regression methods, and SAS/SQL programming. I earned my B.A. in Economics from University of Chicago as well, where I participated in Model UN, The Blue Chips, and Delta Kappa Epsilon.

My hobbies/interests include cycling, vintage audio, rap/hip hop, and augmented reality.
